Best Online Poker Sites Compared 2026

How We Evaluated Each Poker Site

Every site was tested across these criteria with real money play over a minimum of 30 days:

  • Game softness: Average VPIP at various stakes, player pool composition, and win rate potential for competent players.
  • Traffic volume: Peak and off-peak cash game and tournament player counts. Can you always find a game?
  • Software quality: Desktop and mobile client performance, features, multi-tabling support, and customization options.
  • Bonus and promotions: Welcome bonus value, ongoing promotions, rakeback and loyalty programs.
  • Banking: Deposit and withdrawal methods, processing times, fees, and minimum amounts.
  • Customer support: Response time, helpfulness, and available contact methods.
  • Security and licensing: Regulatory compliance, RNG certification, and player fund protection.

GGPoker - Best Overall Online Poker Site

GGPoker has become the dominant force in online poker over the past several years, and in 2026 it holds the top position for good reason. The games are softer than any other major platform, the software is innovative and user-friendly, and the tournament schedule rivals PokerStars with massive guaranteed prize pools.

Why GGPoker Leads

The player pool is significantly softer than PokerStars or PartyPoker. GGPoker invests heavily in marketing to recreational players, and their policies discourage professional grinders. Third-party HUD software (like PokerTracker and Hold'em Manager) is banned. Their built-in Smart HUD provides basic statistics without the overwhelming data that helps regulars exploit casual players. This creates an environment where recreational players last longer and have more fun, which keeps the games soft.

The software includes innovative features like PokerCraft (a built-in hand history analyzer), All-In Insurance (reduce variance on all-in decisions), Deal It Twice (run the board twice to reduce variance), and Staking (built-in staking platform). The mobile app is the best in the industry, making GGPoker the clear leader for players who primarily play on their phone.

Limitations

The HUD ban frustrates data-driven professionals who rely on third-party tracking software. Cash game traffic, while growing, still trails PokerStars at some stakes. Some players find the insurance and staking features encourage gambling behavior rather than skill development. Not available in every country.

PokerStars - Best for Traffic and Game Variety

PokerStars has been the largest online poker site for over two decades, and their traffic volume remains unmatched. At any time of day, at any stakes, in any game format, you can find a table on PokerStars. This liquidity advantage is impossible to replicate and makes PokerStars the only viable option for players at higher stakes or in niche game types.

Why PokerStars Excels

The game variety is unmatched: No-Limit Hold'em, Pot-Limit Omaha (4-card and 5-card), Stud, Razz, Draw games, mixed games (HORSE, 8-Game), and every tournament format imaginable. The software client is the most stable and feature-rich in the industry. Multi-tabling support is excellent. The tournament schedule includes the WCOOP, SCOOP, and daily guarantees that attract players worldwide. The VIP program offers reasonable rakeback for volume players.

Limitations

Games are tougher than GGPoker because the platform attracts more regulars and professionals. The rakeback and loyalty program has been reduced over the years. The welcome bonus is competitive but not the most generous. Software innovation has slowed compared to GGPoker's pace of feature development.

888poker - Best for Recreational Players

888poker positions itself as the friendliest poker room for casual players, and they deliver on that promise. The software is simple and inviting, the games are relatively soft, and the no-deposit bonus lets you start playing for real money without investing anything. If you are new to online poker or play casually, 888poker offers the most welcoming experience.

Strengths

  • No-deposit bonus up to $888 free lets you try real-money poker risk-free
  • Soft games due to the recreational player focus and marketing partnerships
  • Clean, simple software that does not overwhelm new players
  • BLAST sit-and-go tournaments with randomized prize pools create exciting moments
  • Strong mobile app that handles well on both iOS and Android

Limitations

Traffic is lower than GGPoker and PokerStars, especially at higher stakes. Game variety beyond Hold'em and Omaha is limited. The software lacks the advanced features that serious players want. Tournament guarantees are smaller than the top two platforms.

PartyPoker - Best for Tournament Players

PartyPoker has rebuilt itself around tournament poker and now offers some of the best tournament value in online poker. Their partnership with the World Poker Tour brings massive live event satellites and online tournament series with impressive guarantees. The anonymous table format creates softer games by preventing player tracking.

Strengths

  • WPT Online tournament series with multi-million dollar guarantees
  • Anonymous tables prevent player profiling and keep games softer
  • Competitive rakeback program for volume players
  • Live event satellites offering seats to WPT Main Events worldwide
  • Improved software client with better stability and features in 2026

Limitations

Cash game traffic is lower than the top three sites. The anonymous format, while good for game quality, frustrates players who want to study specific opponents. Mobile app, while improved, still trails GGPoker and PokerStars in features and performance.

WPT Global - Best for Soft Games

WPT Global launched as the online poker platform for the World Poker Tour brand and has quickly built a reputation for extremely soft games. The player pool skews heavily recreational, and the average skill level is noticeably lower than other major sites. For players focused on win rate above all else, WPT Global offers the most beatable games in 2026.

Strengths

  • Softest games of any major poker platform in 2026
  • WPT brand attracts casual players from live poker backgrounds
  • Flipout tournaments and unique game formats keep recreational players engaged
  • Generous welcome bonus and ongoing promotions
  • Growing tournament schedule with WPT-branded events

Limitations

Traffic is the lowest among sites on this list, which means limited game selection at off-peak hours. Software is still maturing and lacks some features available on established platforms. Higher stakes games may not always run. Long-term player pool sustainability remains uncertain.

Best US-Legal Online Poker Sites

US players in regulated states have different options. The regulated market is separate from the global player pool, which means smaller but legal and protected games.

  • PokerStars US (NJ, PA, MI): Best software, best traffic among US-regulated sites. The PokerStars brand brings the most players.
  • WSOP.com (NJ, NV, PA, MI): World Series of Poker brand with cross-state player pools. Best for tournament players seeking WSOP circuit events and bracelet opportunities online.
  • BetMGM Poker (NJ, PA, MI): Growing platform backed by MGM Resorts. Soft games from the sportsbook and casino crossover player base.
  • Borgata Poker (NJ): Shares the PartyPoker network. Solid games, good promotions, reliable software.

Multi-state compacts between New Jersey, Nevada, Michigan, Pennsylvania, and Delaware allow shared player pools on some platforms, significantly improving traffic and game availability for US players.

How to Choose the Right Poker Site

For Beginners

Start with 888poker for the no-deposit bonus that lets you play free, or GGPoker for the soft games and intuitive mobile app. Both platforms make it easy to learn without being overwhelmed by professional grinders.

For Cash Game Players

GGPoker offers the softest games at low and mid stakes. PokerStars is essential for higher stakes where traffic matters. Playing on multiple sites simultaneously is common among serious cash game players to always have access to the softest available games.

For Tournament Players

PokerStars for the largest guarantees and most diverse schedule. GGPoker for competitive guarantees with softer fields. PartyPoker for WPT-connected events and live satellites. Playing tournaments on all three platforms maximizes your opportunities.

For Mobile Players

GGPoker has the best mobile experience by a significant margin. The app is fast, feature-rich, and designed for mobile-first play. PokerStars mobile is solid but clearly designed as a companion to the desktop client rather than a standalone product.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best online poker site in 2026?

GGPoker is the best overall for most players due to softest games, innovative features, and strong tournament schedule. PokerStars is best for traffic volume and game variety. The best choice depends on your priorities: game softness (GGPoker), traffic (PokerStars), free entry (888poker), or tournaments (PartyPoker).

Is online poker legal in the US?

Online poker is legal in New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Michigan, Nevada, West Virginia, Connecticut, and Delaware with state-regulated platforms. Players must be physically located within the state. Unregulated offshore sites accept US players but operate in a legal gray area.

Which online poker site has the softest games?

GGPoker and WPT Global have the softest games among major sites. GGPoker bans third-party HUDs and markets aggressively to recreational players. WPT Global's newer player pool skews heavily casual. Both offer significantly more beatable games than PokerStars.

What is the best poker site bonus for new players?

888poker's no-deposit bonus (up to $888 free) is best for trying poker risk-free. For depositing players, WPT Global's 100% up to $1,200 is the largest. GGPoker and PokerStars both offer 100% up to $600. Choose based on your deposit amount and expected play volume.

Can you make money playing online poker?

Yes, but it requires skill, discipline, and bankroll management. Most players lose money. Winning requires extensive study, thousands of hands monthly, conservative bankroll management, and continuous improvement. Low-stakes games are beatable with solid fundamentals and sufficient volume.
